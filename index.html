Our Mission

Founded in Kenya, OPCF-Africa exists to serve the most vulnerable among us — the elderly who have been forgotten, abused, or abandoned.

We act where the world looks away.

To combat violence against elderly persons in Africa — establishing rescue centers, conducting research, advocating for their dignity and rights, and partnering with churches and institutions to build a future where the aged are honored.

Our Purpose

To mobilize the Church and civil society to protect and uplift elderly persons across Africa through compassionate, faith-driven intervention.

Our Vision

A continent where every elderly person lives in dignity, safety, and belonging — beginning in Kenya, expanding across Africa.

Who We Are

A Foundation
Built on
Compassion

OPCF-Africa is a Christian humanitarian organization committed to the protection, care, and restoration of elderly persons across Africa. We exist at the intersection of faith, justice, and service.

Violence against the elderly — physical, emotional, economic, and spiritual — remains one of Africa's most neglected crises. Millions of elderly men and women face abandonment, exploitation, and abuse in silence. We refuse to look away.

Rooted in the biblical call to honor and protect the vulnerable, we partner with churches, academic institutions, and civil society organizations to establish rescue centers, train caregivers, conduct critical research, and advocate for lasting systemic change.

Beginning in Kenya, we are building a movement — one community, one church, one rescued life at a time — across the entire African continent.

What We Do

Four Pillars of
Transformative Action

01

Rescue Centers

Establishing safe havens across Africa where elderly persons who have experienced violence, abandonment, or exploitation can find refuge, care, and restoration.

02

Discipleship & Training

Providing education, discipleship formation, and entrepreneurial skills — empowering elderly persons to live with purpose, dignity, and economic sustainability.

03

Research & Documentation

Conducting rigorous research on the scope of violence against the elderly in Africa to inform evidence-based policy, church engagement, and community intervention.

04

Advocacy & Rights

Championing systemic change through policy advocacy, public education, and institutional partnerships that enshrine the dignity and rights of elderly Africans.

Our Vision

Starting in Kenya.
Reaching All of Africa.

What begins in Kenya is a seed for a continent. OPCF-Africa is building a scalable model for protecting elderly persons — designed to be replicated across every region of Africa through local church partnerships, trained advocates, and community rescue centers.

We envision a networked movement across all 54 African nations — where no elderly person faces violence, poverty, or abandonment alone.
